Wind Speed

   Wind speed was a surprisingly calm statistic. Besides the few outliers, the wind across the globe seemed to be calm with the reading. The highest recorded speed came in at 41.39 MPH in the town of Berlevag, Norway. This town was extremely north (80.5924 degrees) and along the Arctic Sea. So not only was the wind strong that day, I imagine it was also quite nippy to boot! On the low end, no town recorded a perfect 0 MPH winds in this record. Instead, the low was .25 MPH from the rural town of Zhiganski, Russia along the Lena River.
